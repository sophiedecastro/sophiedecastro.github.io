Some of the main contributing factors to voter supression in the APIDA community include the following:

language barriers: The Voting Rights Act permits interpreters for Limited Enligsh Proficinecy voters, but states and poll workers may limit who can become an interpreter and who can use them

low voter turnout: Despite being the fasted growing community the US, the APIDA community has shown a low voter registration rate (~56%) and low voter turnout rate among eligible voters (under 50%) in recent elections

candidate media party outreach: A 2012 survey revealed that only 31% of Asian Americans reported contact from candidates or political parties

mail in voting: 15% higher than average vote-by-mail rejection rate for Asian Americans in California (2017)

voter purging: Inidvidual state policies tend to flag and threathen the standing of Asian Americans on voter rolls
