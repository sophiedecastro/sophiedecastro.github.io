some of the main contributing factors to voter supression in the APIDA community include:

language barriers: the Voting Rights Act permits interpreters for Limited English Proficiency voters, but states and poll workers may limit who can become an interpreter and who can use them

low voter turnout: despite being the fasted growing community the US, the APIDA community has shown a low voter registration rate (~56%) and low voter turnout rate among eligible voters (under 50%) in recent elections

candidate media party outreach: a 2012 survey revealed that only 31% of Asian Americans reported contact from candidates or political parties

mail-in voting: Asian Americans in California faced a 15% higher than average vote-by-mail rejection rate (2017)

voter purging: individual state policies tend to flag and threathen the standing of Asian Americans on voter rolls

cultural influences: some Asian Americans immigrating from countries without democractic elections might not be accustomed to the practice of voting and political invovlement
